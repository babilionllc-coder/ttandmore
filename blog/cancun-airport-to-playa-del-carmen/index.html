Cancun Airport to Playa del Carmen: Best Transfer Options Compared (2026)

By TT & More Team April 15, 2026 33+ years of experience in Cancun transportation

Getting from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen is one of the first decisions you will make when planning a trip to the Riviera Maya. The good news is you have several options, from affordable ADO buses starting at $12 USD to comfortable private transfers at $80 USD. The distance is roughly 68 km (42 miles), and the drive takes between 50 and 70 minutes depending on traffic and your chosen mode of transport. In this guide, we break down every available option so you can pick the one that fits your budget, group size, and comfort level.

Quick Answer: Distance, Time & Cost

Distance: 68 km (42 miles) from Cancun International Airport (CUN) to central Playa del Carmen.

Travel time: 50 to 70 minutes by road, depending on traffic and stops.

Cost range: $12 USD (ADO bus) to $80 USD (private transfer for up to 6 passengers).

Best value for groups: A private transfer with TT & More at $80 USD total means just $13 per person for a family of six, with door-to-door service and no waiting.

The route follows the well-maintained Cancun-Tulum Highway (Federal Highway 307), a divided toll road that runs parallel to the Caribbean coast. There is no train or ferry between the airport and Playa del Carmen, so all options are road-based. Let us walk through each one in detail.

Shared Shuttle ($25–$35 USD per person)

A Cancun to Playa del Carmen shuttle is a mid-range option where you share a van with other travelers heading in the same direction. Several companies operate shared shuttles from the airport, and you can typically book them online or at the kiosks inside the terminal.

Pros

  • Cheaper than a private transfer if you are traveling solo or as a couple
  • Air-conditioned vehicles
  • Usually depart every 30 to 60 minutes

Cons

  • You must wait until the van fills up or until the next scheduled departure
  • Multiple hotel drop-offs mean the ride can take 90 to 120 minutes
  • Less comfortable with luggage from other passengers filling the vehicle
  • For 3+ travelers, the total cost often exceeds a private transfer

Shared shuttles work fine for budget-conscious solo travelers who do not mind the extra time. However, once you have two or more people in your group, a private transfer at $80 USD total becomes the better deal.

ADO Bus ($12–$15 USD per person)

The ADO bus is the cheapest direct option from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen. ADO is Mexico's premium bus line, and the airport-to-Playa route is one of their busiest.

Where to find the ADO bus at Cancun airport

The ADO bus counter and departure point is located just outside the arrivals hall in both Terminal 2, Terminal 3, and Terminal 4. Look for the ADO signage after you exit customs. You can purchase tickets at the counter or at the automated kiosks. Buying online in advance through the ADO website or app is also possible, though not always necessary as seats are usually available.

Schedule and frequency

ADO buses run approximately every 30 to 60 minutes between 8:00 AM and 10:00 PM. The first buses of the day start around 8:00 AM, and the last departure is typically around 9:30 to 10:00 PM. If your flight arrives outside these hours, you will need an alternative. The ride takes about 60 to 75 minutes with the bus stopping at Puerto Morelos and sometimes Cancun Downtown before reaching the ADO terminal in Playa del Carmen on 5th Avenue.

Pros

  • Lowest price at $12–$15 USD per person
  • Comfortable seats with air conditioning
  • No advance booking required for most departures

Cons

  • Fixed schedule means you may wait up to an hour for the next bus
  • Drops you at the ADO terminal in Playa, not at your hotel — you will need a taxi from there ($3–$5 USD)
  • No service for late-night arrivals
  • Luggage space is shared and can be tight during peak season

The ADO bus is a solid option for solo travelers on a budget who arrive during daytime hours and are staying near 5th Avenue. If your hotel is on the outskirts of Playa del Carmen or in Playacar, add another $3–$5 for a local taxi from the ADO station.

Airport Taxi ($70–$90 USD)

Official airport taxis are available outside the terminals at Cancun airport. These are regulated vehicles with fixed zone-based pricing. A taxi from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen typically costs $70 to $90 USD depending on demand and the specific taxi cooperative.

Things to know

  • Prices are per vehicle (not per person), similar to a private transfer
  • You cannot negotiate the fare — it is set by the taxi union
  • Payment is cash only in most cases (USD or MXN)
  • No advance booking or flight monitoring — you join the queue
  • Uber and Lyft do not operate at Cancun airport due to local regulations

Airport taxis get the job done, but for a similar price you get significantly better service with a pre-booked private transfer. With TT & More at $80 USD, you get a named driver waiting at arrivals, flight tracking, and secure online payment — advantages that the taxi queue cannot offer.

Colectivo / Shared Minivan ($3–$4 USD per person)

Colectivos are the local shared minivans that run between Cancun and Playa del Carmen. They are extremely cheap at just $3–$4 USD per person, but there is a catch: colectivos do not pick up passengers at the airport.

To use a colectivo, you would first need to take a taxi or bus to Cancun Downtown and then find the colectivo departure point on Avenida Tulum. This adds time, cost, and complexity to your journey, especially with luggage.

When colectivos make sense

  • You are already in Cancun Downtown or the Hotel Zone
  • You are traveling light (backpack only)
  • You are comfortable navigating local transit in Spanish
  • You want the most authentic local experience

For most tourists arriving at the airport with suitcases, colectivos are not a practical option for the airport-to-Playa route. They are better suited for day trips between cities once you are already settled in the Riviera Maya.

What to Expect on the Drive

The highway from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen is a well-maintained, four-lane divided road. Here is what you will see along the way:

  • First 15 minutes: You will leave the airport zone and merge onto Highway 307 heading south. The road passes through flat, scrubby Yucatan jungle on both sides.
  • 20–30 minutes in: You will pass the turnoff for Puerto Morelos, a charming fishing village worth visiting during your trip. The highway continues straight and smooth.
  • 40–50 minutes: Suburban development begins to appear as you approach the outskirts of Playa del Carmen. You may see signs for Playacar and the gated resort communities.
  • 50–60 minutes: Your driver will exit the highway and navigate the local streets to your specific hotel or accommodation in Playa del Carmen.

The drive is entirely on paved road. There is one toll booth along the way (around $3 USD, typically included in your transfer price with TT & More). The route is considered safe and is heavily patrolled by federal highway police.

Tips for First-Timers

  1. Book your transfer before you fly. Arriving at Cancun airport without a plan means navigating aggressive timeshare salespeople in the terminal. Having a driver arranged in advance lets you walk past them confidently.
  2. Share your flight details. When you book with TT & More, provide your airline and flight number. We monitor flights in real time and adjust pickup times if your plane is delayed.
  3. Bring small bills. If you plan to tip your driver (appreciated but not mandatory), having small USD or MXN bills is helpful. A $5–$10 USD tip is generous for a Playa del Carmen transfer.
  4. Know your terminal. Cancun airport has four terminals. Most international flights from the US and Canada arrive at Terminal 3 or Terminal 4. Your TT & More driver will meet you at whichever terminal you land in.
  5. Avoid the timeshare vendors. After customs, you will pass through a gauntlet of people offering "free" transportation in exchange for attending a timeshare presentation. Politely decline and look for your driver or the official taxi/bus area.
  6. Download offline maps. Cell service is reliable along the highway, but having Google Maps or Maps.me downloaded offline gives you peace of mind to track your route.
  7. Consider round-trip booking. If you know your departure date, booking both your airport-to-Playa and Playa-to-airport transfers together ensures seamless logistics for your entire trip.
  8. Pack a light layer. The air conditioning in vehicles and at the airport can be surprisingly cold compared to the tropical heat outside. A light sweater in your carry-on keeps you comfortable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen?
The drive from Cancun International Airport (CUN) to Playa del Carmen takes approximately 50 to 70 minutes depending on traffic and your exact destination within Playa. A private transfer is typically faster because there are no additional stops along the route. During peak holiday periods (Christmas, Easter, spring break), allow an extra 15 to 20 minutes for heavier traffic on Highway 307.
What is the cheapest way to get from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen?
The cheapest option directly from the airport is the ADO bus at $12–$15 USD per person. Colectivos (shared minivans) cost just $3–$4 USD, but they do not depart from the airport — you would first need to get to Cancun Downtown. For groups of three or more, a private transfer at $80 USD total is actually cheaper per person than most shared shuttles.
Is there an Uber or Lyft from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen?
No. Uber and ride-sharing apps do not operate at Cancun International Airport due to local regulations and agreements with the taxi unions. Your options are private transfers (pre-booked), shared shuttles, the ADO bus, or official airport taxis. We recommend booking a private transfer in advance to guarantee availability and pricing.
Can I book a private transfer from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen in advance?
Yes, and we strongly recommend it. Booking in advance with TT & More guarantees a driver will be waiting for you at the arrivals hall with a personalized sign, eliminates any wait time, and locks in your price at $80 USD for a private vehicle accommodating up to 6 passengers. You can book online and pay securely through PayPal or Mercado Pago.
Is it safe to travel from Cancun airport to Playa del Carmen at night?
Yes, the Cancun–Playa del Carmen highway is a well-maintained toll road that is safe to drive both day and night. It is one of the most traveled routes in the Yucatan Peninsula. For late-night arrivals, we recommend booking a private transfer rather than relying on the ADO bus (which stops running around 10 PM) or trying to find a taxi. TT & More provides 24/7 airport pickup service.
